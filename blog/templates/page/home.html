Abstract

Brain has many functions such as control motor function, interpret information and control body movement. It is essential to improve the brain functionality. One way to improve the brain’s functionality is by performing superbrain yoga. Superbrain yoga is an exercise that involves squatting. This squatting activity requires special position to energize and recharge the brain’s functionality. Thus, the objectives of this research study are to analyse the change of brain signal and body composition before and after performing squatting activity and determine optimal number of squatting cycles as well. The methodology of this study composed of electroencephalogram (EEG) experiment and EEG signal analysis. EEG experiment is conducted by using 32 channel EEG system. The data collected is analysed using MATLAB and Python software. Alpha band (8Hz~13Hz) in EEG signal is extracted as this band is the main concern in this study. This is because alpha band is corresponds to mental relaxation state. Power Spectral Density (PSD) values obtained from the analysis are compared between after squatting and before squatting activity. Body composition is analysed using bioimpedance analysis (BIA) as well by comparing phase angle between after squatting and before squatting activity. The differences between PSD values of EEG signal before and after squatting are calculated. Most of the results show increasing tendency pattern. The results showed that PSD values for 14 cycles squatting is the highest value as compared to other cycles. On the other hand, phase angle obtained from BIA for 14 cycles squatting shows the highest values as compared to other cycles. In conclusion, 14 cycles squatting is the best cycle to improve brain’s functionality than other cycles.
